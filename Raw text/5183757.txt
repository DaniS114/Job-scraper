Job description, responsibilities and duties
The Project Manager (PM) is responsible for leading a project from its inception to completion throughout the projects stages as defined by PMI methodology: initiation, planning, monitoring & controlling, executing and closing. PM’s responsibilities consist of, but are not limited to, managing the people, resources and scope of the project. PM’s overall duty is to create clear and attainable objectives and to see them through to successful completion. The project manager has full responsibility and authority to complete the assigned project.
Responsibilities:
Ensure customer requirements are met by applying standard (PMI) project management principles, support it by knowledge of ordering, provisioning and installation processes of both, customer and its suppliers’ products
Proactively resolve issues that challenge project schedule, scope, cost or quality and manage changing needs and priorities of the client’s end-customer
Maintain the identified standard of formal project documentation and prepare executive overviews as necessary
Manage all aspects of the end to end delivery of services to customer
Direct the completion of activities such as architecture design, engineering, change control, and operations acceptance to ensure complete success of delivering services to the customer
Act as a request dispatcher and accept, reject or delegate received requests
Compile reports and assist with ad-hoc program set up
Assist in creating the detailed plan and schedule, hold responsibility over regular tracking of the project progress against the project plan
Create and adjust the project plans as applicable
Maintain a close contact and interlock the project plan with the project stakeholders
Provide project reporting to the involved customer
Lead enablement activities (possible sub-projects) and associated responsibilities which are part of a wider over-all project enablement
Provide (sub-project) reporting
Ensure deliverables are within customer’s requirement as specified in Statement of Work
