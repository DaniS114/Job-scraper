Information about the position
Job description, responsibilities and duties
️ CME Group is seeking a strategic and technically accomplished Group Head of Network Architecture & Operations to lead the transformation of our global network infrastructure.
️ This role defines the long-term network strategy, drives modernization roadmaps, and manages budgets to ensure scalable, secure, and high-performance connectivity across CME’s international footprint. The position covers enterprise and cloud networking, as well as infrastructure supporting media platforms such as Video on Demand (VoD).
️ Broadcast experience is an advantage but not required. Frequent travel across CME’s territories will be essential to foster collaboration and maintain strong local engagement.
Who we are? https://www.cme.net/
What You Will Be Responsible For?
Strategic Leadership & Planning
• Define and execute the global network architecture strategy aligned with CME’s business, cloud, and media transformation goals.
• Develop and maintain a multi-year modernization roadmap, setting clear milestones for infrastructure evolution and service delivery.
• Lead and coordinate a virtual team of network professionals across CME’s territories, promoting collaboration, innovation, and accountability.
Cloud & VoD Networking
• Design and optimize hybrid connectivity across public and private clouds (e.g., Azure, AWS, O2 Cloud), including secure interconnects, VPNs, and SD-WAN overlays.
• Support VoD platform infrastructure, including CDN integration, high-availability clusters, and web delivery optimization.
• Partner with cloud architects and platform teams to ensure seamless integration of network services with SaaS, PaaS, and IaaS environments.
Infrastructure & Operations
• Manage high-capacity, low-latency transport networks (e.g., OTN/DWDM) for enterprise and media operations.
• Oversee IP management, VLAN segmentation, and multicast configurations across data centers and cloud environments.
• Ensure disaster recovery readiness and cross-border failover for critical services including VoD and broadcast systems.
Support Operations
• Oversee and enhance delivery of L1–L3 network support services, ensuring stability and performance of business-critical systems.
• Maintain clear escalation paths, ownership, and SLA adherence in line with ITIL practices.
• Collaborate with IT, Broadcast, Cybersecurity, and Online units to ensure consistent support coverage and knowledge transfer.
Budgeting & Resource Management
• Own and manage the global network infrastructure budget, including cost forecasting, optimization, and investment planning.
• Evaluate and prioritize network initiatives based on ROI, strategic alignment, and risk assessment.
• Manage vendor relationships, contracts, and SLAs to ensure quality and cost efficiency.
Innovation & Future-Proofing
• Evaluate and integrate emerging technologies such as SDN, network automation, and cloud-native networking.
• Drive innovation while supporting CME’s ESG goals through energy-efficient network design and sustainable practices.
Security & Compliance
• Implement and maintain strong network security controls, including firewalls, VPNs, NAC, and Zero Trust Architecture.
• Collaborate with CISOs to align network security design with CME’s cybersecurity strategy.
• Ensure compliance with internal governance and relevant industry standards.
• Promote best practices for Change, Incident, and Problem Management in line with ITIL.
Media & Broadcast Support (as applicable)
• Support modernization of media workflows, including IP-based broadcast infrastructure (e.g., SMPTE 2110) and cloud playout.
• Provide architectural guidance for video transport, ensuring reliable, low-latency content delivery.
• Collaborate with broadcast engineering teams to enable UHD and remote production capabilities.
• Optimize multicast routing, synchronization (e.g., PTP), and bandwidth provisioning for high-quality video distribution.
