About the Role
NEXUS is Swiss Re’s strategic technology platform for Enterprise Data Exchange that we use to offer a centralized, integrated, and standardized way to exchange data between applications. Over the last 12 years we have developed NEXUS with customized features that are used by all Swiss Re’s clients. We offer you the chance to join the dynamic and agile team responsible for the development and support of the group wide application NEXUS. You bring new ideas and can broaden our experience-base in emerging technologies and the latest software engineering trends.
In this role as Java Engineer, you will:
•	Collaborate closely with our business to understand and address engineering requirements
•	Participate actively in solution design and implementation processes
•	Deliver high-quality work by applying standard methodologies
•	Continuously improve and maintain existing applications
•	Ensure compliance with Swiss Re security requirements
•	Offer technical expertise and guidance to business analysts and other team members
About the Team
As part of Swiss Re's Group Data Services organization, we work in a scaled agile model with a shared leadership team and broadly aligned autonomy for our squads. We are located across the globe but share the same values of passion, openness, respect, humility, and curiosity in our team. We strive to improve the quality of our work for our customers every day. As a matter of trust, we give you a high degree of flexibility which matches your independent working style. Is this for you? Then we want to get to know you!
The main focus for the team is the development and support of the Group Information Broker. The purpose of the application is to exchange data between publisher and subscriber(s) within well-defined SLA's. The meta-information details to process the publisher/subscriber data is defined in contracts. The application takes care of the consuming the data from the publisher endpoint, execute a structural data validation and data transformation before it is stored on the subscriber endpoint. This is a critical component of the global IT infrastructure as well as being a cornerstone of the Data Governance strategy. Examples of upcoming priorities include enhancements to support clients in the private and public cloud, adding connectors for new data sources types, implementation of new features
