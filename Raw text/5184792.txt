About the Role
Do you want to grow one of the best engineering communities and deliver reliable platforms at scale? We’re looking for a
Chapter Lead
to coach our DevOps & SRE engineers, define standards and toolchains, and partner with squads to raise automation, performance, and reliability across cloud-native environments.
As Chapter Lead, you’ll drive both our engineering practices and people development within the
Automation & Orchestration
platform. You’ll lead a motivated, high-performing international team while shaping strategy and practices that support Deutsche Telekom’s transformation. The role includes occasional international travel (~20%).
Key Responsibilities
Leadership & People
Mentor and coach chapter members in both technical and professional growth.
Define career paths, set objectives, and provide regular feedback.
Foster a collaborative, inclusive, and motivated team culture.
Strategy & Development
Define and own the DevOps/SRE vision aligned with platform strategy.
Drive adoption of best practices (AI, CI/CD, Infrastructure as Code, automation, testing).
Continuously develop technical expertise and awareness of new technologies.
Collaboration & Global Impact
Partner with regional teams, deliver training, and represent the chapter at global events.
Act as a trusted advisor across international engineering communities.
Required Skills & Experience
Hands-on expertise in DevOps & SRE (AWS/GCP/Azure, Kubernetes, Terraform, Prometheus, ELK/EFK).
Strong grasp of system design, networking, and security principles.
Proven track record in automation, distributed systems, and incident response.
Experience building and leading high-performing engineering teams.
Excellent communication skills, able to engage both technical and business stakeholders.
Strategic mindset with ability to balance vision with operational demands.
International perspective, adaptability, and fluency in English (other languages a plus).

Growth Mindset

Collaboration
: You overcome silos, build trust, and communicate transparently.
