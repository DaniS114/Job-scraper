Job description, responsibilities and duties
Are you an embedded systems test expert with deep experience with C language? Do you want to apply your skills to a critical mission in healthcare?
Our company, a leader in medical diagnostic technology, is building a new team in Slovakia. We develop life-saving laboratory diagnostic equipment, and we are looking for a talented engineer to help us redefine the intelligence and robustness of our systems
https://www.siemens-healthineers.com/sk/laboratory-diagnostics.
Your Profile
Must have skills:
Experienced in C programming for embedded systems
Deep knowledge of CAN Bus systems (both generic and proprietary variants). Experience with handling bus overflow, message prioritization, and unpredictable behavior is crucial
Hands-on experience with hardware testing and knowledge of NI LabVIEW, NI TestStand and NUnit
Nice to have skills:
Proven experience with microprocessor/firmware development, preferably with TI microcontrollers (c2000, Hercules, ARM R5F, M33, and M85)
Experience with low-level communication protocols like SPI, I2C, UART, etc.
Experience with hardware interfaces ADC, DAC, motor controls, sensors, etc.
Understanding of MathWorks Simulink and Stateflow
Experience with CI/CD would be a strong advantage
Solid general electrical knowledge, especially in control systems and feedback loops
The Problems You Will Solve:
You will become the first member of our Slovakia-based team, collaborating closely with colleagues in the US and India to shift system intelligence from high-level Windows platforms down to the device control level (DCL) and ensure high quality and stability of the system.
Your responsibilities will include:
Applying your expertise in stress and boundary testing to create exceptionally fault-tolerant systems
Developing automated regression tests and test suites to ensure robustness of the system
Expanding our use of CAN bus for all internal sensor communication, ensuring stability and preventing system shutdowns from bus overflow
Designing and implementing robust firmware for subsystems that can operate immediately upon power-on, before the full OS boots
Contributing to our mission to deliver high end innovative healthcare products to people all over the world
This role is an excellent fit for skilled engineers from the automotive industry or any other field with a strong emphasis on robust, real-time embedded systems and CAN bus communication.
